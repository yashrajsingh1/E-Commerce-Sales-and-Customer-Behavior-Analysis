## Project Title for Resume
**E-Commerce Sales & Customer Behavior Analysis** | Python · SQL · Power BI · Excel

## Bullet Points (choose 4–6 per role)

### Data Analysis & Insights
- Analysed 1M+ e-commerce transactions using Python (Pandas) to identify a 25% YoY
  revenue growth driver and seasonal peaks, enabling data-backed Q4 inventory planning.

- Engineered 7 new features (total_sales, hour, quarter, etc.) and resolved 15%
  data quality issues (nulls, duplicates, cancellations) to produce a clean,
  analysis-ready dataset.

- Conducted exploratory data analysis producing 8 visualisations that surfaced
  actionable insights on peak shopping hours (10–12 AM) and top revenue days (Thursday).

### Customer Segmentation & CRM
- Built an RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) segmentation model classifying 4,300+
  customers into 8 behaviour groups, directly informing a targeted re-engagement
  campaign strategy for At-Risk customers (estimated recovery of £120K revenue).

- Identified that Champions (top 8% of customers) contribute 38% of total revenue,
  leading to the recommendation of a VIP loyalty programme.

- Discovered that 63% of customers placed only one order, surfacing a retention gap
  addressed by proposing an automated post-purchase email series.

### SQL & Reporting
- Wrote 20+ SQL queries (CTEs, window functions, JOINs) to answer business questions
  on revenue trends, geographic performance, and customer behaviour.

- Automated a monthly KPI summary report (Python + SQLite) reducing manual reporting
  effort by an estimated 3 hours per week.

### Power BI Dashboard
- Designed a 3-page interactive Power BI dashboard with 15+ visuals and dynamic slicers,
  delivering a self-service analytics tool for marketing and operations stakeholders.

- Defined 9 KPIs (Total Revenue, AOV, Repeat Rate, Customer Count, etc.) and
  implemented DAX measures for real-time metric calculation.

### Business Communication & Stakeholder Value
- Translated complex RFM segmentation outputs into plain-English segment profiles
  and a prioritised action plan for the marketing team.

- Produced a geographic concentration finding (UK = 85% of revenue) that supported
  the business case for international market expansion into Germany and France.
